Are you ready for the challenge?"

Magic Language Rules

🧪 Rule 1: Comparing Things

Use -er than to compare two things, and the -est for the top one!

  • The Giant is bigger than the Elf.
  • Hermione is the smartest witch in class.
  • Maths is more difficult than Art.

📜 Rule 2: What is it made of?

To describe magic objects:

  • The wand is made of wood.
  • The cauldron is made of metal.
  • The ebook is made of plastic and glass.

⏳ Rule 3: The Pensieve (Past Simple)

Irregular verbs change their form when we talk about yesterday:

  • Go → Went (Harry went to the library).
  • See → Saw (I saw a ghost yesterday).
  • Have → Had (We had a Maths exam).

Your Team Mission

Your team must create a "Magic School Catalog". You need to include:

1. The Object List

Draw and name 5 objects: Scissors, Dictionary, Bin, Ebook, Website. Write what they are made of.

2. The Subject Battle

Compare your subjects! Which is better, harder, or more interesting? (Maths vs Art vs History).

3. Yesterday's Diary

Write 3 sentences about what your team did yesterday using the Past Simple (went, saw, ate, had).

The Step-by-Step Plan

Follow these steps to complete your scroll:

  • Step 1: Search for the vocabulary in your book. Find: Geography, History, Maths, Art.
  • Step 2: Use your Dictionary to find more materials (wood, metal, paper, plastic).
  • Step 3: Draw a Bin and a Website on your poster.
  • Step 4: Write your comparisons: "My ebook is newer than my old book."
