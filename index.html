Urgent: The Muggle World is Leaking!

"Attention Wizards and Witches,

Artifacts from the Muggle world are appearing in Hogwarts! We've found things called 'scissors', 'ebooks', and even a 'website'. We don't know what they are or what they are made of.

Your mission is to create a 'Comparison Guide' to explain our world to them and understand theirs. You must also write down what happened during this discovery yesterday.

Good luck, the future of the Statute of Secrecy is in your hands!"

— Arthur Weasley, Office of Muggle Artifacts

Spells of Language

🧪 Comparing Magic

When two things are different, we use -er than. For the #1, we use the -est.

  • Maths is harder than Art.
  • History is the most interesting subject!
  • Harry's wand is better than Ron's old wand.

📜 Materials: "Made of"

Describe what things are built with:

  • The bin is made of metal.
  • The dictionary is made of paper.
  • The ebook is made of plastic and glass.

Your Creation: The Explorer's Scroll

In your groups, you will create a large poster (or digital scroll). It must have 3 sections:

Part 1: The Muggle Artifacts Case

Draw 4 objects: scissors, bin, dictionary, ebook. Label them and write a sentence for each using "made of".

Example: "This is a bin. It is made of plastic."

Part 2: The Hogwarts Subject Tournament

Compare 3 subjects: Maths, Art, History, Geography. Write 4 sentences using comparatives and superlatives.

  • Compare two (better than, smaller than...).
  • Choose the absolute winner (the best, the most interesting...).

Part 3: The Secret Diary (Yesterday)

Write a short paragraph (4-5 lines) about what your group did yesterday at Hogwarts. Use at least 3 irregular verbs in the past (went, saw, had, ate, drank, wrote).

The Step-by-Step Mission

1. Assign Roles: Choose a Lead Illustrator (draws), a Grammar Wizard (checks spells), and a Time-Keeper.

2. Investigation: Use your dictionary or the school website to find the names of materials (wood, metal, glass, plastic, paper).

3. Draft: Write your sentences on a scrap of paper first! Check the past of irregular verbs.

4. Presentation: Prepare to read your Scroll to the class. Use your best wizard voice!
